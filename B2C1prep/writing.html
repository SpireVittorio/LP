Writing

Writing exercise: an article (B2)

You have read this advertisement in an English magazine:

How do you stay motivated?
When was the last time you aimed to do something difficult and succeeded in it?
What do you think helped you achieve it?

Write your article in 140-190 words. Send your review by email to spire.vittorio@gmail.com for feedback.

Writing practice: an essay (B2)

In your English class you've been talking about present and past lifestyles. Your English teacher has asked you to write an essay and answer the following question:

Was life better 50 years ago than it is now?

You should write about:
1. health
2. free time
3. your own idea

Write your essay in 140-190 words. Send your review by email to spire.vittorio@gmail.com for feedback.

Writing practice: a review (B2 & C1)

You read this in an English website:

REVIEWS WANTED.

Have you stayed in a hotel recently? What was it like?
Tell us where, what activities it had for guests and your opinions on it, good or bad.

Write your review in 140-190 words (B2 level) / 220-260 words (C1 level). Send your review by email to spire.vittorio@gmail.com for feedback.

Writing practice: a report (B2 & C1)

Your town has recently organized a music festival and the city hall is asking your school to evaluate its performance.

You should write a report explaining what the music festival was like, the facilities it provided for guests and list some suggestions to improve the music festival for next year.

Write your report in 140-190 words (B2 level) or 220-260 words (C1 level). Send your report by email to spire.vittorio@gmail.com for feedback!

Writing practice: an essay (C1).

Your class has taken part in a seminar on whether the education system does enough to help young people to find jobs which fit their abilities and interests. You have made the notes below:

Methods schools and universities use to help students find suitable jobs.
*providing courses and qualifications.
*work experience programmes.
*careers advice.

Some opinions expressed in the seminar:
'We study lots of things which we'll never use in any future job.'
'Without work experience you'd have no idea what to study at university.'
'Some of my teachers can't give me advice because they've never done any job other than teaching.'

Write an essay discussing two of the methods in your notes. You should explain which method is more effective, giving reasons in support of your answer. You may, if you wish, make use of the opinions expressed in the seminar, but you should use your own word as far as possible.

Write your essay in 220-260 words. Send your answer to spire.vittorio@gmail.com for feedback.

Writing practice: a proposal (C1).

You see this notice on the website for your town or city council.

The local council is planning to honour a local hero connected with your area. The local hero can be someone well known or an ordinary citizen. He/she could still be living or could be someone from history.

Residents are invited to send in proposals identifying a deserving person, giving reasons for their choice and suggesting a suitable way in which this hero should be honoured.

Write your proposal in 220-260 words. Send your text to spire.vittorio@gmail.com for feedback.
